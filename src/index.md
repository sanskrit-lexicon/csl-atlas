## The central finding

PWG named six kośa sources as `<ls>H.</ls>` (Hemacandra, 17,337×), `<ls>AK.</ls>` (Amarakośa, 14,473×), `<ls>MED.</ls>` (Medinīkośa, 13,055×), `<ls>H. an.</ls>` (Hemacandra Anekārthasaṃgraha, 9,771×), `<ls>TRIK.</ls>` (Trikāṇḍaśeṣa, 8,365×), `<ls>HALĀY.</ls>` (Halāyudha, 5,114×).

**PWK** (Böhtlingk's own 1879–89 abridgement) **dropped the kośa apparatus** — these six labels appear only 0.01–0.59% of entries.

**MW** (1899) **replaced them with a single `<ls>L.</ls>` hedge** — 40,212 citations across **13.95% of all entries**.

The [Lineage Sankey](/tools/lineage-sankey) visualises this collapse as the central evidence for [@gasyoun](https://github.com/gasyoun)'s observation that **"MW is based on PWG, but WIL is based on kośas"** — two different intellectual ancestries colliding at almost the same headword.
