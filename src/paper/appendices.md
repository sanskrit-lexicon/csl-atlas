# Framework appendices A · B · C

The three external-framework treatments, condensed as appendices to the [grounded body](grounded). Each preserves its framework's distinctive contribution; full text in the canonical paper.

📄 **Canonical:** [PAPER.md Appendices A–C](https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/MWS/blob/docs-pass/papers/microanalysis/PAPER.md#appendix-a--the-wiegand-theoretic-reading-condensed).

## Appendix A — Wiegand microstructure

MW has a **fully integrated microstructure at the macro level, additive at the micro level** for thin entries. Three maximally-discriminative structural indicators (`genuineroot`, `<ls>L.</ls>`, `<lang>`); an 18-indicator inventory consistent with a *scholarly all-information dictionary* (the Grimm class). The `L.`-hedge is a deliberate *Strukturveränderung* of PWG's source-attribution system. — [§A](https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/MWS/blob/docs-pass/papers/microanalysis/PAPER.md#appendix-a--the-wiegand-theoretic-reading-condensed)

## Appendix B — Atkins-Rundell practical lexicography

MW is a **retrieval dictionary** (not production): maximally-explicit compound headword inventory (50.4% of records), translation-equivalent glosses, **no examples**, locator-only citations, and `L.` as a koshic-only register marker. Its apparent gaps are coherent under that brief. — [§B](https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/MWS/blob/docs-pass/papers/microanalysis/PAPER.md#appendix-b--the-atkins-rundell-practical-lexicography-reading-condensed)

## Appendix C — Hausmann-Wiegand comment-classes

MW's 18 blocks partition into Form / Semantic / (residual) Pragmatic / Quellen comment-classes — plus a proposed **fifth class, *Provenienz-Komment***, for the `<ls>L.</ls>` hedge that names the *kind* of evidence rather than a source. Fourteen article types reduce to five recurring signatures. — [§C](https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/MWS/blob/docs-pass/papers/microanalysis/PAPER.md#appendix-c--the-hausmann-wiegand-comment-class-reading-condensed)
