# Hausmann–Wiegand hybrid framing

Applying Franz Josef Hausmann's *comment-classes* taxonomy (1977, 1985) extended with Wiegand's refinements to MW1899.

📄 **Full paper:** [paper-hausmann.md in MWS docs-pass](https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/MWS/blob/docs-pass/papers/microanalysis/paper-hausmann.md) (~7K words)

## Distinctive contribution

This paper proposes a **fifth comment-class** — *Provenienz-Komment* (provenance-comment) — to accommodate MW's `<ls>L.</ls>` lexicographer-hedge, which Hausmann's original four-class system (form / semantic / pragmatic / source) cannot place.

The Sankey diagram is the central evidence for the proposed fifth class:

![Sankey](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sanskrit-lexicon/MWS/docs-pass/papers/microanalysis/figures/sankey-en.svg)

## Five recurring signature patterns

The framework yields a fivefold typology of MW article-types: form-dominant (roots) · balanced (standard nouns) · semantic-dominant (compounds/derivatives) · provenance-dominant (lexicographer-only) · encyclopedic-doubled (botanicals/biographicals).
