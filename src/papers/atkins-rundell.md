# Atkins–Rundell practical lexicography framing

A reading of MW1899 through Atkins & Rundell's *Practical Lexicography* (2008) framework.

📄 **Full paper:** [paper-atkins-rundell.md in MWS docs-pass](https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/MWS/blob/docs-pass/papers/microanalysis/paper-atkins-rundell.md) (~7.5K words)

## Central claim

MW is a **retrieval dictionary** — Atkins & Rundell's term for a reference work optimised for the user already familiar with the language; not a production dictionary; not a learner's dictionary. Several of MW's apparently archaic features are *coherent design choices* under this typology, even where A&R's prescriptions for modern dictionaries would reject them.

The article-type treemap shows the composition that this framework analyses:

![Treemap](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sanskrit-lexicon/MWS/docs-pass/papers/microanalysis/figures/treemap-en.svg)

## Distinctive contributions of this framing

- Names the *adjacency-sub-entry* pattern (`<e>1A`) that A&R's framework otherwise misses — a third option between lumping and splitting.
- Identifies the 50.4% compound-enumeration policy as a *deliberate over-enumeration*.
- Shows that MW's lack of examples is **coherent under the retrieval-dictionary brief** rather than a defect.
